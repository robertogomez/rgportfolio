Semi-Autonomous Robotics Research

In addition to undertanding the fundamentals of any displine, I have always believed that it is important to acquire practical, real-life experience outside of the classroom. To that end I was fortunate enough to particapte in the 2012 NSF/MESA Summer Team Internship Program. As a research assistant for the SDSU Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, I was able to develop a semi-autonomous robot with object avoidance capabilities. The purpose of the research was to develop a way for a robot to safely navigate an area by deciding if there are obstructions in its path and making appropriate avoidance manuevers. Basing it on the popular iRobot Create® platform, I used two ultrasonic sensors to provide object detection so that the robot could detect objects in front of it. To interface between the iRobot® platform and the sensors, I programmed an Arduino Mega microcontroller to receive the sensor data, interpret it to determine if there is an obstruction in its path, and send movement commands to the robot through serial communication.

While it was a simple project as far as robotics go, it involved aspects from many different disciplines, such as eletrical, computer, and mechanical engineering. Firstly, I had determine how to conect all the components so that they would all function as a single system. For example, I needed to figure out how to draw power from the iRobot® to distribute to the sensors and microcontroller. Then I programmed the Arduino microcontroller (which essentially uses C/C++ language) to analyze the pulse width output signals from the two sensors, decide if an avoidance manuever is necessary, and send the corresponding movement commands to the iRobot® via serial communication. Lastly, I designed a few parts in SolidWorks in order to provide mounting surfaces for the components. The parts were formed from plexiglass using a CNC machine in the SDSU engineering machine shop.

Looking back, this research has given me a great sense of accomplishment. It has definitely increased my confidence in my ability to face challenges in embedded systems. For more information check out the research poster at the right that my partner and I created (he worked on a website for the Retired Senior Volunteer Patrol). If you would like to know some of the technical details of the robot I can send you the report I wrote for the research.
