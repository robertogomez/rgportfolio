Semi-Autonomous Robotics Research

I have always believed that it's important to acquire practical, real-life experience outside of the classroom. To that end I was fortunate enough to participate in the 2012 NSF/MESA Summer Team Internship Program. As a research assistant for the SDSU Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, I developed a semi-autonomous, mobile robot with object avoidance capabilities. Based on the popular iRobot Create® platform, it used two ultrasonic sensors for object detection. For interfacing between the platform and sensors, I programmed an Arduino Mega microcontroller. It was responsible for interpreting the sensor data, determining if obstructions were in the robot's path, and issuing movement commands.

While it was a simple project as far as robotics go, it involved many disciplines, such as electrical, computer, and mechanical engineering. First, I determined how to connect all the components so that they would all function as a single system. For example, I created a simple circuit on a breadboard that delivered power from the iRobot® to the sensors and microcontroller. Then I programmed the Arduino (which essentially uses C/C++ language) to analyze the pulse width output signals from the two sensors. If an object was detected within ten inches, the microcontroller would send a sequence of bytes to the platform through serial communication to initiate a simple avoidance maneuver that consisted of stopping, rotating a number of degrees, and continuing forward. Lastly, I designed a few parts in SolidWorks to provide mounting surfaces for the components. The parts were formed from Plexiglas using a CNC machine in the SDSU engineering machine shop.

Looking back, this research has given me a great sense of accomplishment. It has definitely increased my knowledge and appreciation for robotics and embedded systems. For more information view the research poster my partner and I created (he worked on a website for the Retired Senior Volunteer Patrol). If you would like to know some of the technical details of the robot I can send you my report for the project.
